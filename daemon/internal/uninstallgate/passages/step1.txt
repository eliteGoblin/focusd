I am choosing to uninstall the very thing I built to protect my focus and
my future. Before I am allowed to do that, I am asked to slow down and be
honest with myself. The urge I am feeling right now is loud, but it is not
permanent. It will fade, usually within a few hours, and when it fades I
will still have to live with whatever I did while it was loud. The reason I
set this up in the first place was that I already knew, in a calm and clear
moment, that this exact moment would come, and that the person I am right
now would try to talk me out of my own commitment. That earlier version of
me did not trust this version, and that distrust was earned. So I am writing
this out by hand, slowly, on purpose, because the act of typing every word
forces me to actually read it instead of skating past it. I am not in
danger. Nothing bad will happen if I keep the protection in place for a few
more hours. The only thing I lose by waiting is the ability to act on an
impulse I have already decided, repeatedly, that I do not want to act on.
If after the full waiting period I still believe, with a steady mind, that
removing this is the right and considered choice, then the system will let
me do it. It is not a prison; it is a delay that gives my better judgment
time to catch up with my willpower. I accept the wait. I am willing to come
back later and continue this process if I still mean it then.
