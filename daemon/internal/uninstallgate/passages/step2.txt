Some hours have passed since the first time I tried to remove this, and I
have come back to try again. That alone tells me something worth noticing.
If the original urge had been a genuine, reasoned decision rather than a
craving, the waiting would not have changed how I feel, and the friction
would simply be an inconvenience on the way to a sound choice. But if the
urge has weakened, or if I had half forgotten about it until I came back
specifically to push it through, then what I am actually fighting is not a
decision at all. It is a habit wearing the costume of a decision. I am
typing this second passage by hand for the same reason I typed the first:
to make the cost of removing my own guardrails high enough that only a
calm, deliberate version of me will pay it. A distracted, impulsive version
of me will not sit here and transcribe paragraphs and wait through hours.
That is the entire point, and I designed it that way knowingly. I will be
honest with myself about why I want this gone. If the reason is that the
protection is genuinely broken or no longer serves me, that reason will
still be true after the next waiting period, and I can act on it then with
a clear conscience. If the reason is that the protection is working exactly
as intended and is now inconvenient, then the inconvenience is the feature,
not the bug, and removing it would be a gift to the version of me I trust
least. I choose to keep waiting. I will return once more, later, and only
then decide.
